Character Counter for SEO: Write Better Meta Descriptions & Titles

Published Jun 16, 2026 · 5 min read

Your meta title and description are the first things a searcher sees about your page. Get them right, and you boost click-through rates. Get them wrong, and Google truncates your carefully crafted copy with an ellipsis. That's where a reliable character counter becomes an essential SEO tool.

In this guide, we'll cover the exact character limits you need to know, how to write compelling snippets that drive clicks, and why character counting matters more now than ever.

Current Google Display Limits (2026)

Google updates its display length for search snippets periodically. As of 2026, these are the limits to target:

Title Tags

  • Display limit: 50–60 characters (roughly 600 pixels wide)
  • Optimal length: 50–55 characters
  • Cutoff behavior: Truncated with "…"

Meta Descriptions

  • Display limit: 150–160 characters
  • Optimal length: 140–155 characters
  • Cutoff behavior: Truncated with "…"
Pro tip: These are display limits, not strict rules. A 62-character title won't always get cut — it depends on the width of the characters used. But why gamble? Use a free character counter tool to check before publishing.

Writing Titles That Get Clicks

Your title tag does three things: it tells search engines what the page is about, it appears as the clickable headline in search results, and it shows up in social shares and browser tabs.

Title Best Practices

  1. Put your primary keyword first. Front-loading your keyword helps both users and search engines understand relevance instantly.
  2. Include your brand at the end. "Best Character Counter | CheatSheet Tools" reads better than "CheatSheet Tools Best Character Counter."
  3. Use active, compelling language. "Write Better Meta Descriptions" beats "A Guide to Meta Descriptions."
  4. Stay under 55 characters. This gives you a safety buffer against pixel-width truncation.

Meta Descriptions: Your Free Ad Space

Google has stated that meta descriptions aren't a ranking factor. However, a compelling description dramatically improves your click-through rate, which does influence rankings indirectly.

Writing Descriptions That Convert

A great meta description includes:

  • The keyword or a close variant — bolded in results when it matches the query
  • A clear value proposition — what will the reader learn or get?
  • A subtle call to action — "Learn how," "Discover," "Get started"
  • A complete thought — don't trail off; end with conviction

Example of a great description (155 chars):
"Optimize your meta titles and descriptions for search engines with precise character counting. Tips for staying within Google's display limits while maximizing click-through rates."

How Character Count Affects Mobile vs. Desktop

Mobile search results show fewer characters per line because the viewport is narrower. A title that fits on desktop may get truncated on mobile. The safest approach:

  • Keep titles under 55 characters
  • Keep descriptions under 150 characters
  • Front-load the most important information

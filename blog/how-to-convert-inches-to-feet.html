How to Convert Inches to Feet (and Back): Simple Math for Daily Life

Published Jun 21, 2026 · 6 min read

Whether you're measuring a piece of furniture, planning a home renovation, or just trying to figure out your height in feet, converting between inches and feet is one of the most common measurement calculations you'll encounter in daily life. Yet it's surprisingly easy to get wrong under pressure.

This guide will show you exactly how to convert inches to feet (and back again) with simple math you can do in your head, a handy reference table, and enough real-world examples to make it second nature.

The Basic Formula: Inches to Feet

The relationship is simple: 1 foot = 12 inches. To convert inches to feet, divide the number of inches by 12.

Inches to feet:feet = inches ÷ 12

For example, 36 inches divided by 12 equals 3 feet. That's your basic calculation.

Dealing with Remainders

Most real-world measurements won't divide evenly. If you're 70 inches tall, dividing by 12 gives you 5.833 feet. That's technically correct, but people don't say "I'm 5.833 feet tall." They say "I'm 5 feet 10 inches."

Here's how to handle remainders:

  1. Divide the total inches by 12 — the whole number is your feet.
  2. Multiply the remainder (decimal part) by 12 — that's your remaining inches.

Example:70 inches ÷ 12 = 5 remainder 10. So 70 inches = 5 feet 10 inches.

Feet to Inches: The Reverse Conversion

Going the other way is even easier. Multiply the number of feet by 12 to get inches.

Feet to inches:inches = feet × 12

Examples:

  • 5 feet × 12 = 60 inches (a common height threshold)
  • 6.5 feet × 12 = 78 inches (6 feet 6 inches)
  • 0.25 feet × 12 = 3 inches (a quarter of a foot)

Real-World Applications

Home Improvement & DIY

When shopping for lumber or flooring, measurements are almost always in feet and inches. A 2×4 stud is actually 92 ⅝ inches long. Need to know how many feet that is? Quick math: 92.625 ÷ 12 = 7.72 feet (or 7 feet 8⅝ inches). Need more help? Use our inches to feet converterfor instant results.

Furniture Shopping

That 84-inch sofa you're eyeing? That's exactly 7 feet. Grab a tape measure and mark 7 feet on your floor to visualize the fit before you buy. For larger measurements spanning multiple units, our unit convertercan handle any conversion you throw at it.

Fitness & Height

In many parts of the world, height is still measured in feet and inches. If you know your height in centimeters (or total inches), converting to feet-inches format helps you fill out forms, create fitness profiles, or just communicate your height clearly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Forgetting the remainder:70 inches is not "5.8 feet" in common usage — it's 5 feet 10 inches. The decimal 0.833 × 12 = 10 inches.
  • Mixing up the operation:Inches to feet is divide. Feet to inches is multiply. They're not interchangeable.
  • Rounding too early:If you're doing multiple calculations, keep the decimal and round only at the end.

Why This Still Matters in a Digital World

Sure, you can pull out your phone for every measurement. But there's real value in being able to do quick mental conversions. It helps you catch errors when a tool gives you an unexpected number, speeds up on-site measurements when your hands are full, and builds genuine numeracy that transfers to other areas of life.

Typing Test Mastery: How to Improve Your WPM Speed

Published Jun 15, 2026 · 6 min read

In our digital-first world, typing speed is a superpower. The average person types at about 40 WPM. Professional typists hit 75+. Some exceed 100. The good news is that anyone can improve with the right approach — and a good typing test is your starting point.

Where Are You Now?

Before you can improve, you need a baseline. Take a free typing test to measure your current WPM and accuracy. Record these numbers — they're your starting point.

Speed Benchmarks

  • Below 30 WPM: Beginner — focus on accuracy and proper technique
  • 30–50 WPM: Average — most office workers fall here
  • 50–70 WPM: Above average — good for most professional roles
  • 70–90 WPM: Fast — competitive for data entry and transcription
  • 90+ WPM: Expert — professional transcriptionist level

Fundamentals of Fast Typing

1. Proper Finger Placement

The home row (ASDF for left hand, JKL; for right) is the foundation. Each finger is responsible for specific keys above and below its home position. If you're still hunting and pecking, this one change will double your speed.

2. Don't Look at the Keyboard

Cover your hands with a towel if you have to. Looking down breaks your rhythm and forces your brain to switch between visual and tactile modes, slowing you down significantly.

3. Accuracy First, Speed Second

It sounds counterintuitive, but focusing on accuracy (98%+) builds muscle memory faster. Speed naturally follows. Each mistake you make is training a wrong movement pattern that you'll have to unlearn later.

Practice Routines That Work

15 Minutes a Day

Consistency beats intensity. Fifteen minutes of focused practice daily will produce better results than two hours once a week. Use our online timer to structure your practice sessions.

Focus on Weak Spots

Most people struggle with the lower row (ZXCVBNM) and numbers. Spend extra time on these. Many typing tests offer category-specific drills.

Use Varied Content

Don't just type the same sentences. Alternate between prose, code snippets, random words, and numbers. This builds versatility and prevents plateauing.

Common Mistakes That Slow You Down

  • Death-gripping the keyboard: Relax your hands. Tension reduces speed and causes fatigue.
  • Inconsistent rhythm: Type at a steady pace. Don't burst and pause.
  • Skipping warm-ups: Spend 2 minutes on easy text before attempting speed runs.
  • Not using all fingers: If your left index finger is doing the work of three fingers, you're limiting yourself.

Tracking Progress

Take a timed typing test every week and log your results. Look for trends, not day-to-day fluctuations. A 3–5 WPM improvement per month is excellent, sustainable progress.
